Educational attainment, income, and homeownership

The figures below show the relationship between educational attainment (percent of population in each state with a high school diploma and bachelor's degree), home ownership rate, and per capita personal income.


Press play to see how educational attainment has improved by state over the time period we evaluated.

The bar height represents total educational attainment for each state (HS diploma plus bachelor's degree rates) and the blue line represents the homeownership rate.


We found that in many cases where educational attainment is high homeownership is low and vice versa. This could be due to urban vs. rural populations, housing availability, and student/transient populations (WV vs. DC)



Bubbles farther to the upper right represent higher educational attainment. Bubble size comes from homeownership rate, and color comes from per capita personal income (scale at right).


As expected higher educational attainment correlates with higher per capita personal income, but does not correlate with homeownership.


The maps below help to illustrate our conclusion. Looking at the first two maps you can see that education and homeownership do not necessarily correlate - areas with lower educational attainment (Mississippi and West Virginia) are among the highest in rates of homeownership. But when we compare the educational attainment map to the median household income map a clear correlation emerges.
